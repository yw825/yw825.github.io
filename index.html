About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Operations & Business Analytics at Drexel University's LeBow College of Business (graduating June 2026), with a Graduate Minor in Computer Science. My research sits at the intersection of privacy-preserving machine learning, multi-objective optimization, and healthcare data science.

My work addresses a fundamental tension: hospitals and research institutions need to share patient data to advance medicine, but doing so raises serious privacy risks. I develop optimization models and practical frameworks that help data owners navigate this trade-off — sharing data in ways that are both analytically useful and privacy-preserving.

Before Drexel, I earned an M.S. in Business Analytics & Information Systems from the University of South Florida (GPA: 3.97) and a B.S. in Information Systems from Jianghan University, China. I am fluent in English and a native Mandarin speaker.

I am currently seeking Research Scientist, Applied Scientist, or Postdoctoral positions in healthcare AI, applied ML, or privacy-preserving data science. I am available from August 2026 and authorized to work in the U.S. on STEM OPT (3 years).
